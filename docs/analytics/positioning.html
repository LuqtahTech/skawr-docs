Positioning

What we are, who it's for, and why the four-pillar combination wins where 14-year-old incumbents already exist.

One-liner

Smart MENA-first product analytics that tells you what's happening — without you asking — and groups your users by how they actually behave.

Category

Product analytics for web + mobile + server apps. Drop-in SDK, event tracking, funnels, retention, journeys. Same category as Amplitude, Mixpanel, PostHog.

We are not:

  • A search platform.
  • A chatbot wrapper on analytics.
  • A generic horizontal tool with no opinion (we ship vertical templates).
  • An open-source project (PostHog already owns that lane).

Who it's for

B2B product / growth teams running web or mobile apps. Two priority segments:

  1. MENA-based product teams (Saudi, UAE, Egypt) where Amplitude / Mixpanel feel foreign — wrong currency, no Arabic, no local payment, data outside region. Empty market.
  2. Global SMB / startup teams burned by Amplitude's MTU pricing — they're punished for retaining users. Wedge globally on price + clarity, even before the MENA story.

The four pillars

1 Proactive smart insights — push, not pull

The system tells you:

  • "Signups dropped 30% yesterday — driven by Android users on iOS Safari fallback."
  • "Users who view pricing twice convert 4× more often."
  • "Step 3 of your onboarding funnel has a hidden drop-off for users from organic search."

No "ask the chatbot" UX. Insights surface in a feed/inbox the moment they're detected. Every insight links back to the underlying chart so the user can verify the math themselves (transparent AI — no black box).

2 Embeddings-powered behavioral clustering

Most analytics tools cohort users by rules you write ("users who did X then Y in 7 days"). That only finds patterns you already suspected.

We embed each user's behavioral journey (sequence of events, properties, timing) into a vector and cluster on semantic similarity. The system surfaces cohorts you didn't know existed: "we found a group of 4,200 users who all hit a specific pre-purchase pattern — they convert 7× higher than average. Here's the pattern."

Reuses embedding infra we already operate (Fireworks via skawr-indexer). Real technical edge — almost nobody in the analytics space does this well.

3 MENA-first

  • Arabic UI with proper RTL layout (not bolted on — designed RTL-first).
  • Hijri calendar option alongside Gregorian.
  • Ramadan / Eid-aware time periodization (don't compare DAU during Ramadan to a non-Ramadan week and call it "down 40%").
  • MENA data residency: AWS me-south-1 (Bahrain) and me-central-1 (UAE).
  • PDPL (Saudi) compliance baked in, not an enterprise add-on.
  • Local pricing in SAR / AED, Mada / Tabby / Tamara as payment options alongside cards.
  • Arabic support, Arabic docs, Arabic onboarding emails.

4 Vertical templates — opinionated for the app type you actually build

Generic analytics ships you an empty canvas. We ship pre-defined funnels, events, dashboards, and benchmarks per vertical:

  • Marketplaces (MVP target): listing-view → contact-seller → message-sent → deal-closed; supply/demand health; seller cohorts; price-band conversion; chat-to-close ratio. Benchmarks calibrated against MENA marketplace norms.
  • Fintech (Phase 2): KYC funnel, first-deposit, first-transaction, churn at 30/60/90, fraud-flag rate.
  • Edtech (Phase 2): course-enrollment → first-lesson → completion; lesson drop-off curves; cohort retention by course.

Vertical templates make a new customer productive in minutes instead of weeks. They also let us anchor benchmarks ("you're in the bottom quartile for marketplace listing-to-contact ratio in MENA") — something horizontal tools can't credibly do.

Why this combination wins

Globally there are 14-year-old incumbents (Amplitude, Mixpanel) with much more product. We don't out-feature them. We out-position them on four axes:

  • Push-not-pull insights — June.so does this for B2B SaaS weekly reports, but nobody does it natively in the core product.
  • Embeddings-powered cohort discovery — essentially nobody in product analytics does this. Real differentiation, hard to copy without an embeddings stack already operating.
  • MENA-first — zero credible local players. Amplitude has no Arabic UI, no MENA region, no local payment.
  • Vertical templates with regional benchmarks — Amplitude / Mixpanel are deliberately horizontal. We start opinionated.

No single competitor sits in even two of these quadrants, let alone all four. That's the moat.

MVP scope vs. long-term vision

The four pillars are the vision. MVP picks a subset:

PillarMVP scope
1. Smart insights MVP Anomaly detection on top metrics + funnel drop-off detection. Deeper insight types come later.
2. Embeddings clustering MVP narrow One auto-discovered cohort surfaced per project per week. Validate the model works before expanding.
3. MENA-first MVP Arabic UI, RTL, Hijri, MENA region, local payment. Non-negotiable from day 1.
4. Verticals MVP marketplace only / Phase 2 fintech & edtech

See MVP Direction for the build order.

What we explicitly do NOT do

  • Natural-language chatbot ("ask Amplitude" / "ask Mixpanel"). Intentional — current LLM Q&A on analytics hallucinates.
  • Open-source / dev-first positioning. PostHog owns that lane.
  • Try to be everything for every vertical at once. We launch with one (marketplaces) and add others only when customers ask.
